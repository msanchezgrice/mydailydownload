My Daily Download — pivot to a career-personalized daily AI newsletter

Web-verified red-team (6 agents) + your locked decisions, turned into a shippable plan.

The Kimi_Agent_AI Skill Tutor MVP/ folder holds a strategy package plus two code skeletons — a React/Vite landing+onboarding app and a Python newsletter engine. The strategy recommends pivoting myaiskilltutor.com to a career-personalized daily AI newsletter as an acquisition wedge. The wedge is real and monetizable — but the MVP as built cannot ship. This plan fixes that and adds a content/SEO growth engine + a ready-to-feed launcher spec package.

01 Verdict

Directionally sound wedge — but three hard blockers and two diligence-killers. The single biggest risk is the trust boundary: the engine emails hallucinated facts under real brand names. That's the actual product (real, cited news) and the top liability. Fix it first, then compliance, then the broken frontend.

02 Top risks (ranked, web-verified)

1Hallucinated news = brand-killer + legal exposureCritical · P0

search_ai_news() asks an LLM (temp 0.5) to invent 2026 headlines, sources ("TechCrunch"), funding ("Harvey raises $300M") and stats ("73% of PMs"). careerContent.ts ships the same fabrications with fake company quotes. Emailing invented facts under real brand names = false attribution / FTC deceptive-content risk.

Fix → invert the pipeline: fetch real items → dedup → rank → LLM summarizes only fetched text with mandatory source links; hard guard rejects any URL not in the fetched set.
2Cannot legally send one emailCritical · P0

Single-checkbox consent; enumerable unsubscribe via raw PK (?id=1,2,3… → anyone unsubscribes anyone); no physical address (FTC ~$53k/email); no consent log; no List-Unsubscribe one-click (Gmail/Yahoo require it); no bounce handling; cold domain sending daily.

Fix → double opt-in token, unguessable unsubscribe token, RFC 8058 header, footer address + privacy link, consent record (ts+IP+text), bounce webhook → deactivate, SPF/DKIM/DMARC + warmup.
3Frontend won't compileHigh

Missing Navbar; imports react-router-dom (only react-router v7 is a dep); pages import 5 symbols careerContent.ts never exports; SampleEmail reads fields that don't exist; three conflicting career-ID namespaces; node_modules never installed.

Fix → rebuild the data layer against the backend slug taxonomy (source of truth); reuse design tokens, theme, markup, leaf components, copy (70–80% salvageable).
4Personalization theaterHigh

Resume tab assigns career via Math.random() presented as "analysis"; backend collects seniority/interests then discards them. Cosmetic loading is fine; asserting a false inference about the user's real data is deception.

Fix → honest, user-correctable role selection; real parsing labeled best-effort; generate at career×seniority granularity so the collected data is actually used.
5Market-claim fragility (kills diligence)High

"Zero career-personalized competitors" is FALSE — Marketing AI Institute, Mindstream (HubSpot-owned), Ben's Bites, Latent Space, Spellbook Redline, SHRM AI+HI already serve career-segmented AI news; Refind (~500k) + Particle do per-interest personalization; The Rundown already shipped role-based onboarding in its University product. Several TAM stats are fabricated/misquoted.

Fix → reframe as "personalization within one product across any career"; stress-test vs The Rundown; drop fabricated stats. Verified-good: sub counts, pricing anchors, CPM $20–50, affiliate 20–40%.

Directory as a map: dailyaibrief.com is a directory of ~15 vertical AI briefs (Sales & Marketing, Enterprise, Legal, Finance…) with a public archive but no RSS/API — ingest via the AgentMail lane or archive-scrape discovery-only; its taxonomy doubles as our category-expansion map (and it's another per-publication competitor — our wedge is per-individual).

Legal pattern (load-bearing): newsletters/directories are a discovery signal only. Always cite the primary source, write 100% original prose, never reproduce a newsletter's blurb/lede/lineup (the AP v. Meltwater line), respect paywalls. AgentMail guards: API-fetch fallback for >1 MB payloads, double-opt-in auto-confirm, Svix verification. Fallbacks: Cloudflare Email Routing, Gmail/Fastmail IMAP.

06 Which categories first

Launch 2–3, not 15 — a cold domain with ~0 subscribers can't warm 15 streams or fact-check 15 daily sends. Criteria: demand × content density × monetizability × low liability blast radius.

1 · Marketing

Highest content density (weekly new AI tools), best affiliate economics (Copy.ai, Jasper, Surfer), large non-technical tool-buying audience.

2 · Product Manager

Validated demand, dense tooling, high-income B2B = premium CPM, shareable how-tos.

3 · Founder

Highest willingness-to-pay / Pro conversion, broad cross-topic tolerance, strong affiliate intent (Stripe Atlas, Mercury).

Next wave: Operations, Sales, Designer, Engineer. Avoid early: Healthcare, Legal, Finance — highest liability blast radius (a fabricated FDA approval / case-outcome stat is dangerous; wrong place to debug a new pipeline).
